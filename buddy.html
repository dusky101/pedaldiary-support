Ride Buddy · Voice chat

Talk to your ride buddy — heard clearly, wherever you ride.

Ride Buddy voice chat is coming to Pedal Diary. It lets two riders who have paired for a ride talk to each other as they go — hands-free, and heard through whatever each rider already uses to listen. I built it for my son, Charlie; this page explains why, and how it stays private.

It is built on the encrypted, peer-to-peer connection Pedal Diary already uses to pair ride buddies. Nothing is recorded, there are no accounts, and it never touches the internet.

  • Off by default

  • No account

Why I built it

It started with Charlie.

Pedal Diary exists because of my son, Charlie. He has complete hearing loss in one ear, so he wears hearing aids that route all sound to his right ear — with a slight delay depending on the direction it comes from. Most of the time that works wonderfully. On a bike it doesn't: wind and passing traffic swallow a voice, and he can't always tell where it came from, so an ordinary chat while riding is almost impossible.

Ride Buddy voice chat fixes that by sending each rider's voice straight into the other's ears. With my AirPods and Charlie's hearing aids — or Charlie's hearing aids and his grandad's — the talking travels device to device, close and clear, instead of fighting the noise of the ride. They can just chat, the way any two riders would.

That is the whole point of the feature: not another gadget to wear, but a friend's voice arriving exactly where each rider hears best.

How it works

Four simple steps on a shared ride

🤝

Invite, then accept

A grown-up turns ride-buddy pairing on for both phones. On a ride, one rider invites and the other accepts — either can decline.

🎙️

Talk as you ride

Once paired, you and your buddy can talk live, hands-free, while you ride side by side.

🦻

Heard your way

The voice plays through the rider's own audio — hearing aids, AirPods or speaker — because it uses the phone's normal sound route.

🔇

Mute or stop, any time

Tap to mute whenever you like. Either rider can end the chat, and it always ends when the ride ends.

The connection

The same private link as ride-buddy pairing.

Voice chat travels over the encrypted, peer-to-peer connection Pedal Diary already uses to pair ride buddies — Apple's MultipeerConnectivity, over Bluetooth and local Wi-Fi. It never touches the internet, has no central server, and isn't linked to anyone's Apple ID.

Because it rides on that existing pairing, it inherits the same controls: it is off by default, a grown-up enables it in Settings → Grown-up only → Ride-buddy pairing on both phones, and either rider can cancel at any moment. The full detail is in the Privacy policy.

Nothing is recorded

The talk is live, and then it's gone.

Ride Buddy voice chat is live audio only. It is never recorded, stored or saved — not on either phone, and nowhere else, because there is nowhere else for it to go. No account, no servers, no internet. When the chat ends, there is nothing left behind.
